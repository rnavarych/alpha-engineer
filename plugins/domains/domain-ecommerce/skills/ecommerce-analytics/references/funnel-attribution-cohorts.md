# Conversion Funnel, Revenue Attribution, and Cohort Analysis

## When to load
Load when tracking conversion funnels, building revenue attribution models, or performing cohort retention analysis.

## Conversion Funnel Tracking

### Funnel Stages
1. **Browse**: product listing or category page view.
2. **Product View**: individual product detail page view.
3. **Add to Cart**: item added to shopping cart.
4. **Checkout Start**: checkout process initiated.
5. **Payment**: payment information submitted.
6. **Purchase**: order confirmed and payment captured.

### Implementation
- Emit a tracking event at each stage with a consistent session/user identifier.
- Use a data warehouse (BigQuery, Snowflake, Redshift) to store raw events.
- Build funnel queries that calculate drop-off rates between each stage.
- Segment funnels by traffic source, device type, customer type (new vs. returning), and time period.

### Identifying Bottlenecks
- High browse-to-product-view drop-off: poor category page design or irrelevant search results.
- High add-to-cart-to-checkout drop-off: shipping cost surprise, forced account creation, or trust issues.
- High payment drop-off: payment form UX problems or insufficient payment methods.

## Revenue Attribution

### Attribution Models
- **Last click**: credit the last marketing touchpoint before purchase.
- **First click**: credit the first touchpoint that brought the user.
- **Linear**: distribute credit evenly across all touchpoints.
- **Time decay**: more credit to touchpoints closer to conversion.
- **Data-driven**: algorithmic models (Markov chains, Shapley values) for credit assignment.

### Implementation
- Track UTM parameters (source, medium, campaign) on landing pages.
- Store touchpoints in a user journey table with timestamps.
- Join touchpoints with conversion events to calculate attributed revenue per channel.
- Use insights to optimize marketing spend allocation across channels.

## Cohort Analysis

### Cohort Definition
- Group customers by acquisition date (weekly or monthly cohorts).
- Track behavior over time: retention rate, cumulative revenue, order frequency.

### Metrics per Cohort
- **Retention**: percentage of customers placing a second order in subsequent periods.
- **Revenue per customer**: cumulative revenue generated by cohort over time.
- **Repeat purchase rate**: percentage of cohort that has ordered more than once.

### Visualization
- Cohort retention matrix (heatmap) to compare cohorts side by side.
- Identify trends: are newer cohorts retaining better or worse than older ones?
- Correlate cohort performance with marketing campaigns or product launches.

## Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)

### Calculation Methods
- **Historical CLV**: sum of all past revenue from a customer minus acquisition and servicing costs.
- **Predictive CLV**: forecast future value using probabilistic model (BG/NBD + Gamma-Gamma).
- **Simple formula**: CLV = Average Order Value × Purchase Frequency × Customer Lifespan.

### Application
- Determine acceptable CAC: ensure CLV > CAC with healthy ratio (3:1 or higher).
- Identify high-value segments for loyalty programs and personalized marketing.
- Allocate retention marketing budget based on predicted future value.
